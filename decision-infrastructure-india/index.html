Why India Needs Decision Infrastructure

ViaDecide Research 5 July 2025 6 min read

India now has one of the world's most sophisticated digital public infrastructure stacks. UPI processes over 13 billion transactions a month. Aadhaar has enrolled over 1.3 billion residents. DigiLocker holds hundreds of millions of documents. ONDC is opening up commerce. The rails are extraordinary.

But there's a gap that no amount of payments infrastructure or identity rails can fix: the gap between having data and making a decision.

The core problem: A farmer in Yavatmal, a student in Patna, a founder in Bengaluru, and an IAS officer in Bhopal all face the same challenge — they have access to more information than ever before, and less ability to synthesize it into a clear decision than ever before. Abundance without synthesis is paralysis.

The synthesis gap

Consider a decision that sounds simple: should a farmer in Vidarbha switch from cotton to soybean this kharif season? To answer that well, you need current MSP data, last three years of price realization, monsoon forecast for Vidarbha, input cost comparisons, state scheme eligibility, and market linkage options. All of this data exists. Most of it is public. None of it is synthesized.

The farmer either relies on their neighbor's heuristic ("last year soy did well"), the village agri-input dealer's incentivized recommendation, or makes no decision at all. The synthesis layer is missing.

This pattern repeats at every level. A first-generation college student choosing between engineering and law. A small manufacturer deciding whether to export. A gram panchayat evaluating a roads contractor. The data exists. The decision architecture doesn't.

India doesn't have a data problem.
It has a synthesis problem.

What decision infrastructure looks like

Decision infrastructure is not a chatbot. It's not a search engine. It's a structured system that: takes a question, queries authoritative sources, synthesizes the results into a coherent brief, identifies contradictions and uncertainties, and presents a decision-ready output — with attribution, so the decision-maker can verify any claim.

This is what policy analysts do for ministers. What consultants do for executives. What research assistants do for academics. ViaDecide makes this capability available to anyone with a smartphone.

The IndiaAI alignment

The IndiaAI Mission's core mandate is "responsible AI for inclusive growth." The emphasis on responsible and inclusive is meaningful. AI that benefits only the already-privileged is not inclusive. AI that hallucinates facts in a health or agricultural decision is not responsible.

Decision infrastructure that is source-attributed, multilingual, grounded in authoritative public data, and accessible on a 2G connection in a rural district — that's what responsible and inclusive AI looks like in practice. That's what we're building.

The path from Vidarbha to Raisina Hill

The same decision infrastructure that helps a farmer choose a crop can help an MLA draft a constituency development brief. The same synthesis engine that helps a student research a topic can help an IAS officer synthesize policy options. The difference is context, not capability.

Decision infrastructure, built right, is not a product for a segment. It's the civic technology layer that every Indian deserves access to — from a village school in Vidarbha to the corridors of Raisina Hill.
